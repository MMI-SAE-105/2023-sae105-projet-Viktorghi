The Memorable Power of Encounters

Spirited Away

In Studio Ghibli's iconic animated film "Spirited Away", an enigmatic quote from Zeniba resonates deeply: “Once You've Met Someone You Never Really Forget Them. It Just Takes A While For Your Memories To Return.”

This line prompts a profound reflection on the nature of encounters and memory. Discover how this maxim applies to our own lives and why it resonates with so many people.

by Viktor Ghirardelli

Published today at 14h45 | Read time 1 min

Encounters that Make an Impression

In life, we are constantly meeting new people, whether friends, colleagues or strangers passing through. Some of these encounters go almost unnoticed, but others leave a lasting impression on our minds.

Zeniba's quote highlights the fact that these encounters, even if they sometimes seem to fade from our memory, always leave a mark. They help to shape who we are and where we've been.

The Fragility of Memory

The second part of the quote, "it's just hard to remember", raises an essential question about memory. Why is it so difficult to remember certain people, even if they have had a significant impact on us? Memory is a complex phenomenon, influenced by many factors, such as the passage of time, stress and the amount of information we absorb on a daily basis.

This part of the quote invites us to reflect on our own memory and the way in which we preserve the memories of people we have met.

The Importance of Memories

Finally, Zeniba's quote highlights the importance of memories and encounters in our lives. Even if we sometimes forget faces and names, the emotional impact and lessons learned from these encounters remain.

Encounters enrich our experience and help us to grow as individuals. It is essential to cultivate gratitude for the people we have met, whether they are still present in our memory or have disappeared into oblivion.

In conclusion, Zeniba's quote from "Chihiro's Journey" reminds us that encounters are a fundamental part of our journey through life. They shape our history and our identity, even if we sometimes find it hard to remember them. It invites us to reflect on how we preserve our memories and to recognise the importance of the people we have met along the way.

So let's take a moment to remember those encounters that shaped our lives, even if they sometimes seem to fade, because, as Zeniba says, you never really forget the people you've met.
